AI Decision-Making in Hiring & Recruitment

An exploration of how AI tools are reshaping recruitment in New Zealand and what this means for fairness, employment and society.

About This Website

This website examines the societal impact of AI decision-making in hiring and recruitment in New Zealand. AI-driven tools are now a major part of how employers select candidates, from automatically screening CVs to conducting video interviews without any human involvement. Our research looks at how these systems work technically, who they affect, and what this means for fairness in the job market.

This topic is directly relevant to us as university students entering the workforce. Many of the casual and entry-level jobs students apply for already use automated screening. As we begin applying for graduate roles, AI will likely be the first to evaluate our applications at most large organisations. Understanding how these systems work, and what risks they carry, is both practically useful and ethically important.

What This Site Covers

Topic

A detailed explanation of what AI recruitment tools are, how they work, and why this topic matters to us as New Zealand students entering the job market.

Opportunities

AI in recruitment can reduce time-to-hire by up to 75%, improve candidate experience, and when designed well, even reduce some forms of human bias in hiring.

Risks

These tools can amplify historical bias, reject candidates at scale with no explanation, and operate in a legal grey area in New Zealand where there is no dedicated AI legislation.

Choices

Organisations face a spectrum of tools from basic CV screening to fully autonomous AI interviews. Each comes with different ethical trade-offs and levels of human oversight.
